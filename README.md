# 1.Data visualization

Data is a raw and unprocessed information.
While Information is the processed data.
To make insightful decisions, we need information to generate patterns to take the necessary actions.
That's where Data Visualization rolls around!
Data Visulaization represents the data graphically with charts.
Main Purpose of Data Visualization: Faster and more accurate information.

There are five steps for beginners(like me) to consider:(crd: my lecturer)
1. Data: Raw materials
2. Information: Processed and organized data(may come in tables)
3. Insights: Patterns(charts and graphs)
   But there are three things to consider:
   i. What is happening now?
   ii. Why is it happening?
   iii. What should we do next?
4. Actions
5. Impact: This is the outcome of the actions you have taken

Why do we use Data Visualization(D.V)?
People are most likely to be engaged in interesting and understandable visuals rather than long ass paragraphs.
Research shows that people are more likely to remember points and facts rather than a whole sentence. In large businesses and corporations, the information one has to handle is mammoth. This will put business owners and people who want quick results in a tight spot.
With graphs and charts, we notice the patterns or proportions or correlations very clearly.
That's why we use D.V for clear insights.

One important thing people don't talk about is that for clear insights and to generate charts, the data itseld needs to be accurate.
Moreover, even if the data is correct, bad representations: overcomplicated visuals, inconsistent colour and data overload are common. They are called common pitfalls.

DATA TYPES(crd: my lecturer)
Three general data types:
1. Categorical
2. Numerical
3. Time-Series

   1. Categorical data types,as the name goes, shows the category of the data (e.g - product type, ethinicity etc)
   2. Numerical data types, as the name goes, applies to things that can be measured. (e.g - age)
   3. Time-Series show the trends overtime(e.g - monthly sales growth)
There are also some categories when it comes to data types:
1. Qualatative
    i. Binary (data with two possible results only: Boolean, gender)
    ii. Ordinal(data with visible ranks: customer satisfaction, app ranks)
   iii. Nominal( data with no rank or inherent color:eye colour, nationality , product types,cars,dogs)
